# Origin Brief Notes

Source: `Organizational AI Memory_ The Capability Layer for the AI Enterprise.docx`

## Category

Do not position OrgMemory as generic knowledge management. The origin brief
positions the category as **Organizational AI Capital Management**.

The atomic unit is an **AI Capability Asset**: a versioned, governed,
transferable combination of prompt logic, workflow configuration, domain
context, and usage evidence.

## Phase 1

Phase 1 is a registry, not passive capture.

Build:

- prompt/workflow submission form,
- structured metadata: owner, use case, department, AI tool, tags,
- simple search and discovery,
- version history,
- basic usage tracking.

Success metric:

- 20 prompts or workflows submitted,
- at least 5 different people actively use them in the demo environment.

## Later Phases

Phase 2 can add ChatGPT and Claude capture, access controls, approval workflows,
and onboarding flows.

Phase 3 can add knowledge graph, maturity scoring, HRIS-triggered offboarding,
and deeper governance.

## Risks To Keep Visible

The biggest risk is the contribution problem. Employees may not voluntarily add
AI workflows to a shared system unless the product solves incentives, privacy,
and trust.

Passive capture can reduce burden, but it creates surveillance and consent
concerns. Do not introduce it before the product has a clear privacy model.
